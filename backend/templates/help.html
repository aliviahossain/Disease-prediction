Help & Frequently Asked Questions

This application helps you understand the probability of having a certain disease based on a positive or negative test result, using Bayesian inference. It's designed to provide an educational tool for understanding probability, not a diagnostic one.

  1. Select a Disease: Choose a disease from the dropdown list.
  2. Enter Prior Probability: This is the general prevalence of the disease in the population. You can adjust it using the slider or input a value directly.
  3. Enter Test Sensitivity: This is the probability that the test correctly identifies someone with the disease (true positive rate).
  4. Enter Test Specificity: This is the probability that the test correctly identifies someone without the disease (true negative rate).
  5. Select Test Result: Indicate whether your test result was positive or negative.
  6. Calculate: Click the "Calculate Probability" button to see the updated (posterior) probability.

  • Prior Probability (Prevalence): The initial probability of a person having the disease before any test is performed. It's the general rate of the disease in the population.
  • Sensitivity: The ability of a test to correctly identify individuals who have the disease (true positive rate). A highly sensitive test will have few false negatives.
  • Specificity: The ability of a test to correctly identify individuals who do not have the disease (true negative rate). A highly specific test will have few false positives.

The Interactive Real-Time Slider allows you to dynamically adjust the "Prior Probability" and see the immediate impact on the calculated posterior probability. As you move the slider, the "Posterior Probability" updates in real-time, providing an intuitive way to understand how changes in prior probability affect the final outcome.

After calculating your disease probability, you can request AI-powered recommendations. This feature uses an advanced language model (like Google Gemini) to generate personalized insights, potential next steps, or general information related to the disease and your calculated probabilities. You can also listen to these recommendations via text-to-speech and change the language.
